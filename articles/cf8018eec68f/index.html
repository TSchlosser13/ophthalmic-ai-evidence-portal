A Hybrid CBAM-Vision Transformer for Enhanced Classification of Retinal Diseases in Optical Coherence Tomography Imagery

Najma Amira Mumtaz, Ardytha Luthfiarta, Adhitya Nugraha, Ika Novita Dewi, Ulima Muna Syarifah, Dzawil Uqul

2025Conference / proceedingsAbstract-only source

Contribution summary

This investigation proposes a CBAM-ViT-tiny hybrid that refines OCT feature embeddings and is trained with full fine-tuning plus weighted loss and augmentation, achieving state-of-the-art test performance on OCTMNIST and thereby offering an effective attention-enhanced modeling strategy for imbalanced OCT classification.

Overall reasoning

In this ophthalmic computer-vision study, a CBAM-augmented Vision Transformer is evaluated for retinal disease classification using Optical Coherence Tomography images, tackling low resolution and class imbalance, with evidence drawn from reported test-set metrics on OCTMNIST.

Abstract

Timely and accurate diagnosis of retinal diseases from Optical Coherence Tomography images is crucial for preventing irreversible vision loss, yet automated classification is challenged by low image resolution and significant class imbalance in public datasets such as OCTMNIST. While Vision Transformers offer a powerful alternative to traditional models, their application to these specific challenges, particularly when enhanced with targeted attention mechanisms, remains underexplored. This research addresses this gap by developing and validating a robust classification model that enhances ViT-tiny architecture with a Convolutional Block Attention Module (CBAM) to improve diagnostic accuracy. Our methodology integrates CBAM to refine feature embeddings, which are then optimized using a full fine-tuning strategy, weighted cross-entropy loss to counteract class imbalance, and on-the-fly data augmentation. The proposed CBAM-ViT model established a new state-of-the-art performance on the OCTMNIST test set, achieving an accuracy of 86.40%, a macro F1-score of 85.97%, and a mean Area Under the ROC Curve (mAUC) of 0.9760, surpassing both a baseline ViT and previously published benchmarks. These findings demonstrate that integrating a CBAM attention mechanism into a fully fine-tuned ViT provides a highly effective strategy for classifying low-resolution, imbalanced medical images, establishing a new, robust performance benchmark and highlighting the value of hybrid attention architectures for challenging diagnostic tasks. © 2025 IEEE.
