Illustrative implementation context

Figure 6 draws on external survey and report sources. These percentages contextualize clinical AI adoption and are separate from the 427 included-study evidence map.

Manuscript caption: Illustrative implementation context for clinical AI from external survey and report sources. (A) Use cases aimed at improving care quality, such as clinical decision support tools, are top priorities, with providers also favoring AI applications such as predictive analytics and workflow optimization . (B) Despite their significant potential, barriers including regulatory and legal considerations, resource constraints, and model accuracy and safety are impeding broader AI adoption, while the need for governance, transparency, and accountability remains substantial . (C) Fewer than half of U.S. adults expect AI to improve patient outcomes in health and medicine, particularly in tasks such as diagnosing diseases and recommending treatments . These data are geographically and institutionally contextual rather than globally representative.
